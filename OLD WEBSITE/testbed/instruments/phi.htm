PHI 660 Scanning Auger Microprobe

This instrument uses a focussed electron beam to excite atoms of the specimen in their inner shells. When these excited atoms decay non-rediatively, Auger electrons are emitted. The Auger electrons can only be distinguished from the backscattered electrons if they come from depths of the order of 1 nm and hence this instrument provides capability for surface analysis. Scanning of the incident electron beam provides the possibility of obtaining a two-dimensional distribution of elements present at the surface at a concentration level greater than 0.1%. With the use of an ion beam to sputter away the surface atoms, the depth distribution of various elements with atomic number greater than 2 can be obtained. The smallest region that can be analyzed is limited principally by the loss of signal as the spot size of the incident beam is reduced. However, in favorable cases, it is possible to utilize spot sizes as small as 50 nm.
