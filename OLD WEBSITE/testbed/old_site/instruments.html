CEMMA INSTRUMENTS

The instruments are used for both research and teaching. Hourly instrument use charges are applied for each instrument according to whether or not the instruments require the use of an operator. Special rates apply for use of a given instrument for more than 10 hours/month and for use of the instruments in collaborative research involving USC faculty and off-campus research projects. The following instruments are included in the Center for Electron Microscopy and Microanalysis and are located in the CEMMA building.

Cambridge 360 Scanning Electron Microscope

The Cambridge 360 has capabilities for image storage and analysis. The microscope has a resolution of 30 angstron and has a Link EDS system. An interface has been designed for the microscope which allows electron beam writing to be done in the SEM.

JEOL 100CX Transmission Electron Microscope

This standard JEOL transmission electron microscope is primarily used for biological specimens. It has a side entry goniometer stage and can be used in bright field or dark field microscopy as well as phase contrast and diffraction contrast microscopy. It has a point-to-point resolution of 0.45 nm and a lattice resolution of 0.2 nm.

Philips EM420 Transmission Electron Microscope

This is an Analytical Electron Microscope (AEM) with capability of operation in the transmission imaging mode with a lattice resolution of 0.2 nm and a point-to-point resolution of 0.34 nm. The Energy Dispersive X-ray Spectrometry (EDS) is capable of detecting all elements with atomic number greater than 2. Electron diffraction is possible both in the Selected Area Diffraction mode (SAD) and the convergent beam electron diffraction mode (CBED). The latter mode provides crystallographic information from regions of the order of 2 nm in size.

Akashi 002B Transmission Electron Microscope

This transmission electron microscope is dedicated for use as a high resolution transmission electron microscope. The point-to-point resolution at 200kV is 1.84 angstroms. The microscope is equiped with a Gatan CCD camera. The microscope allow materials to be studied at the atomic level.

PHI 660 Scanning Auger Microprobe

This instrument uses a focussed electron beam to excite atoms of the specimen in their inner shells. When these excited atoms decay non-rediatively, Auger electrons are emitted. The Auger electrons can only be distinguished from the backscattered electrons if they come from depths of the order of 1 nm and hence this instrument provides capability for surface analysis. Scanning of the incident electron beam provides the possibility of obtaining a two-dimensional distribution of elements present at the surface at a concentration level greater than 0.1%. With the use of an ion beam to sputter away the surface atoms, the depth distribution of various elements with atomic number greater than 2 can be obtained. The smallest region that can be analyzed is limited principally by the loss of signal as the spot size of the incident beam is reduced. However, in favorable cases, it is possible to utilize spot sizes as small as 50 nm.

Rigaku X-ray Diffractometer

The X-ray source is a 12kW rotating anode X-ray generator. A theta-two theta diffractometer with an intrinsic germanium solid-state detector is available for doing routine powder diffraction. There is also a thin-film attachment for doing work at low incidence angles as required for thin-films and a texture goniometer for texture and stress measurements. The entire system is computer controlled with the necessary software for data collection and analysis. There is search/match capabilities and the entire JCPDS file is on CD-ROM.

VJ X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy

In XPS, x-rays bombard a sample creating ionized atoms and ejecting free electrons. The energies of these free electrons are related to their binding energies in the original atom. This is a versatile tool capable of providing both quantitative elemental and chemical states information on virtually any vacuum compatible surface. Sputter depth profiling is also available and detects all elements except H and He.

Gatan Dual Ion Mill

This is an ion beam sputtering unit specifically designed to prepare TEM specimens that cannot be thinned by ultramicrotomy, chemical etching, or electropolishing. It has two stations which can be used simultaneously and has a LN2 cooled stage to minimize specimen heating during the specimen preparation.

Ultramicrotomes

Three Sorvall MT2B Ultramicrotomes are here to use for teaching and research.  Complete tissue processing for SEM and TEM are also available.
