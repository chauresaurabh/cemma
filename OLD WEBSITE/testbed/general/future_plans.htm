RECENT NEWS

September 2011 - THE CENTER ACQUIRES NEW TEM

The Vice President for Research, the Viterbi School of Engineering, and the Dornsife College of Letters, Arts and Sciences have teamed up to provide support for purchasing a transmission electron microscope (TEM). The TEM will be purchased from JEOL JEM-2100F and is an advanced 200kV Field Emission Electron Microscope featuring ultrahigh resolution.

September 2008 - THE CENTER ACQUIRES NEW SEMs

CEMMA has purchased three new SEMs from JEOL with funds from the Provost's Initiative in Biomedical Nanoscience.   Included in this purchase will be a MultiBeam JIB-4500, a two-column SEM-FIB(Focused Ion Beam) for nano-machining, a JSM-7001 low-vacuum field emission scanning electron microscope (FESEM), and a JSM-6610 low-vacuum SEM with tungsten source.  Delivery is scheduled for early 2008.  The Center has also submitted a proposal to NSF for a new 200 kV FE-TEM.

                                   

January 2008 - Renovations

CEM building will undergo extensive remodeling of the "dome" and south side of the first floor, renovating space for laboratories, demonstrations and short courses.   All renovation will be complete before delivery of the new SEMs.
